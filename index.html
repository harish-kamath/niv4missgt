From Niv - Why Me?

What’s up Jackets! This is so surreal - I’m so humbled and honored to be a finalist for Ms. Georgia Tech!!


Tech has been pivotal to my life. Whether through service or leadership or dance or any other pocket of GT I hold close to my heart, I believe that this school and every person I’ve been lucky enough to meet has inspired me to leave this space a little better than I found it.


That’s why my service project is about improving access and resources for cultural and performing arts organizations on campus. Check out my video below for more information on it!

Service Project

These solutions are feasible and have been approved and curated by students and administration alike.


I think it’s unfair that some of our students don’t feel encouraged to represent and celebrate their culture and art. This lack of inclusivity is not what Georgia Tech is about and it boils down to an issue of access.


The biggest goals of this project are to give dance and performing arts teams spaces to practice as well as give cultural groups on campus the resources and tools required to plan events successfully.


I have already:

  • Formed and met with a group of invested campus stakeholders such as Capital Space Management Planning members, Event Logistics Committee members, faculty and staff, etc.
  • Created a comprehensive list of cultural pockets on campus to include with an open space to discuss their issues and needed help.
  • Discussed potential blueprints and logistics for over 5 spaces on campus, outside of athletic facilities, for events and dance spaces.
  • Developed a feasible timeline and strategy for implementation.

But, we need a big circle of champions and advocates to get this done! Cast your vote at tinyurl.com/voteniv so we can catalyze the resources and access this platform provides to make this vision a reality.


By engaging the right people early, I hope to leave Tech with a living gift. Please feel free to reach out with any questions, additional insight or involvement if you’re interested - I’d love to discuss this project further!!

About Niveda

Niveda Shanmugam is a 4th year BS Industrial Engineering and MS Health Systems student from Alpharetta, GA. Niv is passionate about global healthcare access, improving student representation and inclusivity on campus, and giving back to this community that’s inspired her in so many ways.


Anyone that knows Niv knows that she's deeply invested in different pockets of Georgia Tech and loves so much about the pivotal people and opportunities it’s brought her way. She hopes to continue working with the administration and students alike to bring her perspective and build an environment that every student feels genuinely valued and enabled in through her service project. This project will improve access and resources for cultural and performing arts organizations on campus.
