From Niv - Why Me?

What’s up Jackets!! This is so surreal - I’m so humbled and honored to be a finalist for Ms. Georgia Tech and to have received all the love and support I have not only during this process but also my entire time at this school.


Georgia Tech has been pivotal to my life. This school has given me some of the best people, experiences, and memories. I’ve learned and grown so much over the past few years here: whether through service or leadership or dance or any other pocket of GT I hold close to my heart, I believe that this school and every person I’ve been lucky enough to meet has inspired me and all of us to leave every space a little better than we found it - or as we like to call it, progress and service. I know that I’m a better student, friend, leader, and individual because of everything we stand for and embody at this institute. As your Ms. Georgia Tech, I would want to spend my last year here doing everything in my ability to make every Yellow Jacket feel the same support, in a way that’s impactful and tangible.


That’s why my service project is about improving access and resources for cultural and performing arts organizations on campus. Think increased practice spaces or removable flooring for dance teams on campus, sustainable grass for turfs so Tech Green is more accessible, improved communication and resources for events - and so on! Miss GT catalyzes the reach and resources to make that vision a reality, and it’s so exciting that it could come to fruition.


Regardless of what happens, my love for this school will always be infinite - the Yellow Jacket community makes the world a better place and I’m so thankful to be a part of it!! Thank you times infinity - I am endlessly grateful.

Service Project

I think it’s unfair that some of our students don’t feel encouraged to represent and celebrate their culture and art. This lack of inclusivity is not what Georgia Tech is about and it boils down to an issue of access. The biggest goals of this project are to give dance and performing arts teams spaces to practice as well as give cultural groups on campus the resources and tools required to plan events successfully, both with the immense support and intentionality that these organizations need and deserve but are currently lacking.


Overall, students in these smaller organizations struggle with:

  • streamlined communication on real-time updates to all cultural organizations at once, regardless of size and prominence on campus
  • access and ability to resources and conversations about space for events with stakeholders such as CPSM, ELC, Landscaping, FSA or SOFO as opposed to emails
  • comprehensive documentation of all this information for future reference

By engaging the right people early, I hope to leave Tech with a living gift. Please feel free to reach out with any questions, additional insight or involvement if you’re interested - I’d love to discuss this project further!

Already discussed and securing...

  • A group of invested and committed stakeholders on campus such as Capital Space Management Planning members, Event Logistics Committee members, faculty and staff, etc.
  • A comprehensive and growing list of organizations and pockets of campus to include with an open space to discuss their issues and needed help.
  • Potential blueprints and logistics for over 5 spaces on campus, outside of athletic facilities, for events and dance spaces
  • Future meetings and implementation strategy

We need a big circle of champions and advocates to get this done! Cast your vote at tinyurl.com/voteniv so we can catalyze the resources and access this platform provides to make this vision a reality.


But we need your vote to take this all the way!

About Niveda

Niveda Shanmugam is a 4th year BS Industrial Engineering and MS Health Systems student from Alpharetta, GA. Niv is passionate about global healthcare access, improving student representation and inclusivity on campus, and giving back to this community that’s inspired her in so many ways.


Anyone that knows Niv knows that she's deeply invested in different pockets of Georgia Tech and loves so much about the pivotal people and opportunities it’s brought her way. She hopes to continue working with the administration and students alike to bring her perspective and build an environment that every student feels genuinely valued and enabled in through her service project. This project will improve access and resources for cultural and performing arts organizations on campus.
